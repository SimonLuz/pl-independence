FROM THE FLAMES and CHAOS

It’s year 1918. The 1st World War is about to end, the old (European) empires are collapsing, last peace-treaties are being signed, and the new order is about to be built.
The post-war chaos brings an favorable moment for a nation that for almost 5 generations has been erased from the political map of Europe.
On November 11th 1918, following the withdrawal of German forces from Warsaw, Marshall Jozef Pilsudski declares Polish sovereignty and Poland’s Second Republic is born.
The future is wide open.

It’s NOT.

1918 ends 123 years of partitions (by 3 neighbouring superpowers) and that (tumultuous) time has taken a disastrous toll on Polish nation.
Almost 1 million Poles lost their lives during WWI alone, not to mention countless battles, uprisings and emigration that preceded that war.
The economy is ruined, the administration and the army have to be built from the beginning, (and the borders of the newly formed state were questioned and soon to be militarily challenged.).
Yet two even more sinister forces - Nazi Germany and Bolshevik Russia, will soon challenge the young Polish country. As many times before and after, Poland will unfortunately be the first in line to fight them.

If one wants to destroy a nation, they must weaken its spirit and undermine their will to (fight). Polish spirit has never been broken. Despite countless efforts to eradicate polish language and culture and zdusic patriotism, Poles always responded with unparalleled sacrifice and astounding resilience and …………….. . In fact, most of Polish Nobel Prize winners were awarded for their achievements done during the time when Poland was under ……. . Lots of renowned works of literature, music and painting were done during ….., by authors in exhile. (mention Mickiewicz, Chopin, Paderewski, Sklodowska, Milosz ????). Poles were able to maintain their national identity during 123 years of partitions. It was Poland that orginized the largest resistance movement against Nazi Germany during the WWII, and these were Poles that created the largest and organized civil resistance movement during the communist era in the 1970s-1990s. If there is one thing we feel we can be proud of, is the fact that we have never, ever given up. We cannot let this spirit die. We do not want to forget. This is why we march.
