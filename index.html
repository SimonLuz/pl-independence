deeper into the past

It’s year 1918. The 1st World War is about to end, the old (European) empires are collapsing, last peace-treaties are being signed, and the new order is about to be built. The post-war chaos brings an favorable moment for a nation that for almost 5 generations has been erased from the political map of Europe. On November 11th 1918, following the withdrawal of German forces from Warsaw, Marshall Jozef Pilsudski declares Polish sovereignty and Poland’s Second Republic is born.
The future is wide open.

It’s NOT.

1918 ends 123 years of partitions (by 3 neighbouring superpowers) and that (tumultuous) time has taken a disastrous toll on Polish nation. Almost 1 million Poles lost their lives during WWI alone, not to mention countless battles, uprisings and emigration that preceded that war. The economy is ruined, the administration and the army have to be built from the beginning, (and the borders of the newly formed state were questioned and soon to be militarily challenged.). Yet two even more sinister forces - Nazi Germany and Bolshevik Russia, will soon challenge the young Polish country. As many times before and after, Poland will unfortunately be the first in line to fight them. It's 1918, the world is changing rapidly and in the turmoil of post war ..... poland is given a chance Poles have been waiting for 123 years, almost 5 generations - they're getting their country back. The organizational challenges the freshly formed Polish republic has to face are immense, but they're dwarved by the military threat Soviet bolshevik army posed to the country, as well as the entire Europe. Since the collapse of the Eastern Block in 1989 we've regained many of those liberties and we've been exercising most of these rights vigorously. However, we are not free from the legacy of all those years of communistic regime - regime which primary goal was and still is to form a helpless man without free will and without purpose. It takes a lot of time to rebuild our world and ourselves, and we believe that one cannot shape their future without knowing and understanding the past. And this is why we march, this is why we cannot forget.
